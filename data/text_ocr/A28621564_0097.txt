Screened by NARA, 7/28/2023 Name: Roman Avellan, Jose Antonio File: A28 621 564 Page: 3 3. Were you in the National Guard or engaged in fighting on behalf of Somoza's Government? If so, provide details of those activities. (Did you take up arms and participate in any fighting? Was it voluntary or involuntary? Was it in an official capacity?) I was considered a member of the National Guard, but in the Medical Field. I never participated in any fighting, or other military activities. My participation was as Laborating Technician in charge of the Main Penitentiary System. "The Carcel Modelo". I started in '975 under the Administration of Colonel Luis Alfonso Buitrago. Later, Colonel Nicolas Valle Salinas and Colonel Ricardo Lau Castillo, who was the last Commander of the Penitentiary System under the Somoza Government. 4. Provide an outline of your promotion history stating nature and place of your specific assignments. My participation in the military was restricted to the Public Health Field. In 1947 I was promoted to Captain of the Medical Corps. At that time, my father, Doctor Victor Manuel Roman y Reyes, who had been Minister of Public Health in 1931, became President of Nicaragua. I continued in the Medical Corps of the National Guard with the rank of Captain. I was under the administration of many Ministers of Public Health since 1931 : Doctor Heliodoro Robleto, Doctor Emilio Lola, Doctor Jacinto Alfaro, Doctor Pedro Pablo Alvarez, Doctor Alejandro Sequeira Rivas and Doctor Francisco Urcyo Malianos. My assignments were in the city of Managua. I held my position in the Department of Public Health until Somoza left in 1979. 5. Are you related by blood or married to a high government official or a National Guardsman? My father Doctor Victor Manuel Roman Y Reyes who was President of Nicaragua from 1946 to 1950, was also the Commander in Chief of National Guard of Nicaragua, until his death in 1950. Other relatives were member of the National Guard: Colonel Manuel Antonio Roman, Colonel Ramon Torres Roman, Colonel Carlos Silva Reyes. 6. What were your specific duties 12 months prior to the fall of Somoza's Government? 12 months prior to the fall of Somozas's Government, I was working in the Department of Health, in the "Colonia Morazan Center". I worked under Mayor Augusto Morales Urbina, as the Laboratory Technician, in the Tuberculosis Control Department.
